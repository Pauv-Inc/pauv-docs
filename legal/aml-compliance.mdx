Pauv Inc. maintains a comprehensive Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) compliance program designed to prevent the platform from being used to facilitate money laundering or the financing of terrorist activities.

## Closed-Loop Risk Mitigation

Pauv's closed-loop ecosystem fundamentally eliminates standard avenues for money laundering and fraud:

- **No Peer-to-Peer Transfers** — Users cannot send, gift, or trade USDP or PVs to other users
- **No Secondary Markets** — All PV trades execute directly against Pauv's automated pricing curve
- **No External Portability** — USDP and PVs cannot be transferred to external wallets or platforms
- **Closed-Loop Fiat** — Fiat enters via regulated processors and can only be withdrawn back to the verified user's linked bank account

## Identity Verification (KYC)

Pauv enforces strict Customer Due Diligence (CDD) through our compliance partner **Soap Payments**, powered by Plaid IDV and Monitor.

### Tier 1: Standard KYC

Required before depositing funds or creating a Talent profile:

- Data source verification (name, DOB, address, ID number) against authoritative databases
- SMS verification of your mobile device
- Real-time AML screening against global watchlists

### Tier 2: Step-Up KYC

Required for higher-risk activities — includes document verification (government-issued photo ID) and selfie liveness check:

- **Talent withdrawals** — Required before any USD can be withdrawn to a bank account
- **Elevated risk signals** — Triggered automatically when the risk engine detects device anomalies, synthetic identity risk, or low Trust Index scores

## OFAC Screening

Pauv is strictly prohibited from conducting business with individuals, entities, or countries sanctioned by the **Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC)**.

- Every user is automatically screened against the OFAC SDN list and global PEP lists
- Screening is **continuous** — Pauv's user database is scrubbed against daily watchlist updates
- If a match is flagged, the account is immediately blocked, balances frozen, and deposit/withdrawal capabilities disabled
- Required blocking reports are filed with the U.S. Treasury/OFAC within 10 business days

## Geofencing

Pauv is authorized to operate across the United States through BVNK's Money Transmitter Licenses. The platform is **strictly prohibited to international users**:

- All non-US IP addresses are actively blocked
- Account creation is restricted to US-based devices

## Transaction Monitoring

Pauv monitors for suspicious activity patterns including:

- Structuring deposits just under reporting thresholds
- Large fiat deposits followed by immediate withdrawal requests without trading activity
- Rapid deposit-and-withdrawal cycles
- Other red flags specific to the closed-loop model

Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) are filed with FinCEN when warranted.

## Record Keeping

| Record Type | Retention Period |
|-------------|-----------------|
| KYC/CIP data (names, addresses, DOBs, SSNs, document images) | 5 years after account closure |
| Transaction logs (deposits, withdrawals, PV trades) | 5 years minimum |
| Security | AES-256 encryption, role-based access controls |

All transactions are recorded on Pauv's dual-layer hybrid architecture (PostgreSQL + private blockchain), ensuring an immutable, time-stamped, tamper-proof audit trail.

## MSB Registration

Pauv Inc. is a registered **Money Services Business (MSB)** with FinCEN, maintaining a full BSA/AML compliance program including:

- Customer Identification Program (CIP) and Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
- Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) for higher-risk users
- OFAC and sanctions screening
- Transaction monitoring and SAR/CTR procedures
- Employee training on AML/BSA requirements
- Independent testing of AML program and internal controls
