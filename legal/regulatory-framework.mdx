This page provides a high-level summary of Pauv's regulatory classification under U.S. federal and state law. Pauv has obtained a formal legal opinion from qualified outside counsel confirming that the platform operates consistent with applicable legal requirements.

## PVs Are Not Securities

PVs have been analyzed under the **Howey test** (the standard framework for determining whether an asset is a "security" under federal law) and **do not** constitute investment contracts or securities.

### Key Factors

- **No common enterprise** — PV holders do not pool funds into a business venture. Each PV tracks public sentiment about a specific person, similar to a digital trading card.
- **No expectation of profits from others' efforts** — PV prices are set by an algorithmic pricing curve based on buy/sell activity, not by managerial decisions. Talents have no obligation to perform or promote to support PV prices.
- **Collectible nature** — PVs are framed as speculative digital collectibles with entertainment and fan engagement value, not as financial investments. This is consistent with the SEC's 2025 Staff Statement on Meme Coins.
- **No equity or income rights** — PVs confer no ownership, voting rights, dividends, profit participation, or claims on any person's income or assets.

## Not Subject to CFTC Registration

Pauv's activity is limited to **fully funded, spot transactions** in PVs using internal USDP credits. The platform does not offer:

- Futures, options, swaps, or perpetual contracts
- Leveraged, margined, or financed transactions
- Derivatives of any kind

Therefore, CFTC registration requirements do not apply. Pauv's transparent pricing curve and closed-loop design further mitigate fraud and manipulation risks.

## FinCEN — Money Services Business

Pauv is registered with FinCEN as a **Money Services Business (MSB)** and maintains a full BSA/AML compliance program.

**USDP** is classified as **closed-loop stored value** — not a convertible virtual currency (CVC). USDP:

- Cannot be transferred between users
- Cannot be sent to external wallets
- Has no extrinsic monetary value
- Functions solely as an internal medium for platform transactions

However, because Pauv's fiat-in/fiat-out model involves receiving and transmitting funds on behalf of users, it properly operates as a registered money transmitter.

## State Licensing

Pauv's fiat-in/fiat-out model triggers **money transmitter licensing** requirements in most U.S. states. Pauv is pursuing state-level licensure as required.

At the state level, PVs are **not considered securities** in any U.S. jurisdiction because they:

- Lack ownership rights, dividends, or enterprise-related profit expectations
- Derive value solely from market sentiment

## Not a Securities Exchange, Broker-Dealer, or ATS

Because PVs are not securities, Pauv does not operate as:

- A securities exchange
- A broker or dealer
- An alternative trading system (ATS)

Pauv operates a proprietary trading venue for non-security digital collectibles. All orders execute against the continuous linear pricing curve within Pauv's own system — there is no user-to-user matching, bid/ask spread, or linkage to national market systems.
